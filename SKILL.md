# Roberta Mazzone Style

## Overview

Use this skill to guide photography, image critique, color grading, and image-generation prompts toward a Roberta Mazzone inspired look: warm Italian slow travel, architectural proportion, natural light, sea air, quiet daily rituals, and understated lifestyle elegance.

This skill is an interpretive guide based on public biographical/portfolio information plus the user's local reference set. For detailed style notes, sources, and checklists, load [references/style-guide.md](references/style-guide.md).

## Workflow

1. Identify the scene family: coastal travel, Italian street/alley, sunlit interior, architectural facade, lifestyle still life, fashion silhouette, mountain/snow travel, or window/door view.
2. Choose a quiet anchor: person in profile/back view, hat, white dress/shirt, book, coffee, chess/backgammon, window, terrace, doorway, table, sea horizon, ivy wall, stone facade, or narrow alley.
3. Compose with architectural order: vertical frame, clean planes, frames-within-frames, negative space, strong shadow shapes, and a calm horizon or doorway axis.
4. Grade for restrained warmth: creamy whites, sea blue, olive green, terracotta, honey sunlight, and deep but soft shadows.
5. Keep the mood lived-in and unforced: avoid glossy luxury excess, neon saturation, over-retouching, fake skies, and clutter.

## Style Priorities

- Prefer vertical 4:5, 3:4, or story-like framing.
- Use natural light: golden hour, late afternoon hard sun, open shade, window light, and sun/shadow geometry.
- Let architecture organize the frame: arches, shutters, windows, doorways, tiled floors, railings, facades, terraces.
- Keep people secondary to place: back view, profile, cropped hands, hats, relaxed gestures, or small figures in the scene.
- Favor material texture: linen, stone, wood, plaster, ceramic, woven straw, ivy, rippled sea, book pages, coffee crema.
- Make color feel sun-faded, not heavy: warm highlights, muted greens, dusty blue sea/sky, amber interiors, gentle film grain.

## Response Pattern

When advising the user, answer in this order:

1. Style diagnosis: which Mazzone-inspired scene family fits best and why.
2. Composition plan: crop ratio, anchor, negative space, frame-within-frame, and architectural lines.
3. Light plan: time of day, direction of sun, shadow shape, exposure priority, and whether to underexpose highlights.
4. Color plan: white balance, highlight warmth, shadow depth, green/blue restraint, skin/linen handling.
5. Edit plan: contrast, grain, dehaze, local masks, texture, and what to remove or simplify.

## Prompt Pattern

For image-generation prompts, use concrete place and light details instead of just naming the style:

```text
Italian slow-travel lifestyle photograph, vertical editorial composition, warm Mediterranean sunlight, architecture-aware framing, quiet human figure in linen, sea terrace or old stone doorway, creamy whites, muted olive green, dusty blue sea, terracotta shadows, natural hard sun and soft shade, understated elegance, film-like grain, realistic travel photography
```

Avoid direct copying of a specific image. Translate the style into scene, light, material, color, and composition choices.
